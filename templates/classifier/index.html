Welcome to APSI — the Automated Political Stance Identification Tool.

APSI provides a theory-driven and transparent approach to identify political positions in texts. The tool uses pre-trained language models to to estimate where texts fall along key political dimensions, including economic ideology, support for liberal democratic values, and populist versus pluralist rhetoric. APSI focuses on linguistic patterns and provides probabilistic scores. Our goal is to support research, journalism, non-governmental organization and anyone interested in the critical exploration of political discourse.

Scores range from 0 to 10. Low and high values represent different ends of each dimension.

Economic Left–Right

Role of the state and markets.

0: redistribution, welfare, regulation
10: privatization, low taxes
Support for Liberal Democracy

Democratic institutions and rights.

0: weak institutional support
10: strong democratic commitment
Populism–Pluralism

Rhetorical framing style.

0: pluralist, institutional
10: populist, anti-elite

APSI uses Natural Language Inference (NLI). The model evaluates whether your text supports a set of theory-based political statements and aggregates these signals into stance scores.

In plain terms

We ask “Does this text imply X?” for multiple political statements, then combine the answers into a score.

Political language is nuanced and context-dependent. Results can be sensitive to wording, sarcasm, and missing context.

  • Sarcasm and irony
  • Very short texts
  • Quoting opponents
  • Strategic political language

This tool is for research and informational purposes only. Do not use it for profiling, surveillance, or decisions affecting individuals.
