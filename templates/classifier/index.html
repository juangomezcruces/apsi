What the tool does

The Automated Political Stance Identifier (APSI) is a tool designed to analyse texts and identify the possible political stances they express. It uses a hypothesis-based approach grounded in political science theories to evaluate whether a text reflects specific political stances, such as economic left–right orientation, populist versus pluralist rhetoric, and support for liberal democratic values. Instead of relying on manually labeled training data, APSI applies a Natural Language Inference (NLI) model to test how strongly a text supports or contradicts a set of predefined hypotheses. By aggregating these results, the tool produces interpretable stance scores, confidence measures, and supporting evidence, enabling researchers, policymakers, and the public to systematically evaluate political discourse in a transparent and reproducible way.

The dimensions we measure

Economic Left–Right Scale

It captures positions ranging from strong state intervention, redistribution, and welfare provision to free markets, privatization, and a limited role for government.

Support for Liberal Democracy

It captures the level of commitment to free and fair elections, political competition, and fundamental rights such as freedom of speech, media, assembly, and association expressed in the text.

Populism–Pluralism Rhetoric

It captures whether the text emphasizes the will of “the people” against elites and institutions, or instead supports institutional constraints, and minority rights.

How the analysis works

The APSI tool uses a theory-driven Natural Language Inference (NLI) approach to analyse texts. Instead of relying on keywords or labeled training data, the tool evaluates how strongly a text supports or contradicts predefined political statements in the form of hypotheses derived from political science theories. It first checks whether a text is relevant, then estimates its position across key dimensions — such as economic ideology, populist versus pluralist rhetoric, and support for liberal democratic values. These results are combined into transparent scores, confidence estimates, and supporting evidence.

Limitations

APSI provides estimates based on patterns in language and should be understood as an analytical support tool rather than a definitive judgment of political beliefs or intentions. The tool evaluates text in isolation, without contextual knowledge about the author, audience, or broader circumstances, and its results depend on the theoretical hypotheses related to the corresponding dimension. Outputs may reflect limitations in training data and model design, and therefore require careful human interpretation.

Ethical considerations
This tool is for research and information only. Like any AI-driven tool, it can be biased due to its training data and how it is designed. It should not be used for profiling, surveillance, or making decisions about people. Users are responsible for the content they submit, including ensuring compliance with copyright and other applicable legal requirements.
