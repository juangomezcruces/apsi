{% empty %}
Direct classification

A single-step estimate of stance along each dimension. Useful as a quick baseline.

Hypothesis-based scoring

The model evaluates multiple left/right (or pluralist/populist) hypotheses, aggregates probabilities, and maps the difference to a 0–10 score.

Response-based scoring

The model compares the text to anchored category descriptions (e.g., Left=0, Center=5, Right=10), then computes a weighted average.

Does APSI “know” what the author believes?

No. APSI analyzes language in the text and estimates alignment with dimensions. It does not infer identity, intent, or personal beliefs.

Why do results change when I rewrite a sentence?

The model is sensitive to wording. Small phrasing changes can strengthen or weaken cues used for scoring.

Can the tool detect sarcasm or quotes?

Not reliably. Irony, sarcasm, and quoted statements are common sources of misclassification.

What text length works best?

Longer passages with clear policy or institutional language tend to yield more stable scores.
